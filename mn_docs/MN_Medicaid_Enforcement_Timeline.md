# Minnesota Medicaid Fraud: Timeline of Warnings and Enforcement

This timeline contrasts the early warning signs (audits, county complaints) with the delayed administrative and law enforcement actions, illustrating the cost of reactive oversight.

## Early Warnings & Precursor Events
* **2003:** OLA audit finds DHS has not made controlling improper payments a priority.
* **2007:** OLA financial audit of HCBS finds DHS does not collect adequate financial documentation from providers.
* **Feb 2017:** OLA issues major evaluation of HCBS Financial Oversight. Warns that DHS relies on reactive complaints, lacks routine financial audits, and notes that 10% of providers account for 70% of payments. Recommends extending strict hour caps and documentation rules to all direct-care workers.
* **2017:** Minnesota becomes one of the first states to offer Medicaid coverage for EIDBI (autism) services.
* **2019:** OLA special review of Child Care Assistance Program (CCAP) finds DHS did not adequately identify fraud risk and had weak investigative processes statewide.
* **Jul 2020:** Minnesota launches Housing Stabilization Services (HSS), the first state to offer Medicaid coverage for this service.
* **2021:** Integrated Community Supports (ICS) program begins.
* **2023:** HHS-OIG inspects the MN MFCU (covering FY2020-2022) and notes the unit's director is the only supervisor, limiting oversight capacity.

## The Fraud Escalation & Ignored Warnings (2023–2024)
* **Oct 2023:** Hennepin Health (county MCO) begins receiving feedback about HSS providers aggressively approaching members and offering money to sign up.
* **Dec 7, 2023:** MN AG announces charges against three individuals for nearly $11 million in Medicaid fraud (the Promise Health Services case, operating out of a mailbox).
* **Mar 15, 2024:** Hennepin Health sends its first written complaint to DHS regarding Start Today Hennepin (STH), flagging identical, cloned service notes for every client.
* **Apr 2024:** County emails document HSS providers offering $50 cash bribes to homeless individuals for referrals.
* **May 6, 2024:** Hennepin Health SIU Manager emails DHS OIG regarding kickbacks. A DHS OIG manager replies: "We're flying blind on this so far..."
* **Jun 19, 2024:** MN AG announces charges against five individuals for >$10 million in fraud (Promise Health Services update and Driving Miss Daisy NEMT case).
* **Jul 2024:** CMS issues its FY2023 Focused Program Integrity Review Final Report, identifying non-compliance in how Minnesota MCOs handle payment suspensions and fraud referrals.
* **Jul 2024:** Hennepin Health makes an official fraud referral to DHS for STH, documenting falsified records and billing for deceased clients.
* **Aug 2024:** Hearth Connection publishes a report on predatory HSS enrollment practices.
* **Oct 2024:** DHS finally begins EIDBI provider audits and on-site visits.
* **Nov 2024:** DHS OIG manager emails: "We all want to avoid housing services becoming the next poster child for government program fraud..."
* **Dec 2024:** Hennepin County begins banning multiple HSS providers; DHS allows them to continue billing other insurers.

## The Crisis & Administrative Crackdown (2025)
* **Jan 2025:** Then-DHS Commissioner Jodi Harpstead tells a Senate hearing there are more "rumors of fraud" than actual fraud. She leaves her post shortly after.
* **Feb 2025:** Gov. Walz appoints Temporary Commissioner Shireen Gandhi to lead DHS.
* **Mar 2025:** DHS hires a new Inspector General, James Clark.
* **May 1, 2025:** KARE 11 publishes its first investigation into HSS fraud.
* **May 2025:** DHS officially designates EIDBI and HSS as "high-risk" services, triggering additional scrutiny. Legislation passes enacting stricter controls.
* **May 30, 2025:** HHS-OIG issues an audit finding that Minnesota failed to ensure CCAP providers complied with attendance requirements, resulting in improper payments.
* **Jul 2025:** State and federal agents raid 8 HSS-associated locations.
* **Aug 2025:** DHS announces the decision to terminate the HSS program effective Oct 31, 2025.
* **Sep 18, 2025:** First wave of federal HSS charges: IRS-CI announces wire fraud charges against 8 defendants for defrauding HSS.
* **Oct 2025:** DHS designates 9 additional Medicaid services as "high-risk" (bringing the total to 14).
* **Oct 2025:** DHS terminates 761 inactive provider agencies. Establishes pre-payment review and contracts with Optum for third-party claims data analytics.
* **Oct 31, 2025:** HSS program is officially shuttered due to rampant fraud.
* **Dec 5, 2025:** CMS demands a Corrective Action Plan (CAP) from Minnesota.
* **Dec 18, 2025:** IRS-CI announces charges against 6 additional defendants (HSS and EIDBI) and a guilty plea in the EIDBI scheme. A search warrant is executed regarding ICS fraud.
* **Dec 2025:** DHS pauses new licensing for HCBS and adult day care settings for two years.
* **Dec 31, 2025:** Minnesota submits its initial CAP to CMS.

## The Federal Threat & "Revalidate 2026" (2026)
* **Jan 6, 2026:** CMS denies Minnesota's initial CAP and threatens to withhold up to $2 billion in Medicaid funding.
* **Jan 6, 2026:** OLA releases a performance audit of DHS Behavioral Health Administration Grants, citing fabricated documentation, overpayments, and multiple unresolved prior findings.
* **Jan 14, 2026:** Federal Register publishes a notice of opportunity for a hearing on Minnesota's program integrity compliance.
* **Jan 2026:** DHS freezes provider enrollment for 13 Medicaid services.
* **Jan 30, 2026:** Minnesota submits a revised CAP.
* **Feb 25, 2026:** Federal officials announce a plan to defer $259 million in Medicaid funding from Minnesota.
* **Mar 19, 2026:** CMS approves Minnesota's revised CAP.
* **May 2026:** The Medical Assistance Protection Act (MAP Act) is signed into law, giving the MFCU stronger legal tools.
* **May 21, 2026:** **The Minnesota Health Care Fraud Takedown.** DOJ announces charges against 15 defendants for >$90 million in fraud across EIDBI, ICS, IHS, HSS, and CCAP.
* **May 31, 2026:** Deadline for "Minnesota Revalidate 2026." DHS completes the revalidation of ~5,583 high-risk providers. ~3,400 are disenrolled (mostly for incomplete data or failed site visits).
* **Jun 9, 2026:** DHS publishes final outcomes of the revalidation process.
* **Jun 23, 2026:** **National Health Care Fraud Takedown Day.** MN AG announces charges against 7 providers for >$700,000 in false claims. DOJ announces national charges against 455 defendants, explicitly citing the Minnesota takedown as the catalyst for the national Medicaid enforcement expansion.
